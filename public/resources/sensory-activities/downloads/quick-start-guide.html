Sensory Activities Quick Start Guide

Getting Started with Sensory Activities

Welcome to your journey of supporting your child's sensory development! This guide will help you understand and implement effective sensory activities at home.

Quick Tips:

  • Start slowly and observe your child's reactions
  • Choose activities based on your child's interests
  • Make it fun and playful
  • Be consistent but flexible

Essential Materials Checklist

  • Sensory bin (large plastic container)
  • Various textured materials (rice, beans, sand)
  • Play dough or modeling clay
  • Soft brushes and sponges
  • Balance beam or tape for floor lines
  • Squeeze toys or stress balls
  • Musical instruments or sound makers
  • Light-up toys or fairy lights

Daily Sensory Schedule Example

Time Activity Duration
Morning Heavy work activities (pushing, pulling) 10-15 mins
Mid-morning Fine motor activities 15-20 mins
Afternoon Sensory bin exploration 20-30 mins
Evening Calming activities 15-20 mins

Next Steps

  1. Create a dedicated sensory space in your home
  2. Gather materials from the checklist
  3. Start with one activity and gradually add more
  4. Keep a journal of what works and what doesn't
